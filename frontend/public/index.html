02 — The Truth

Banned
Everywhere
Except
Here.

The EU, UK, Japan, Australia — they banned these ingredients years ago. The US food industry kept using them because it's profitable and nobody forced them to stop. Until now, most people had no idea they were eating them.

Red Dye 40
Found in: Doritos · Fruit Loops · M&Ms · Jell-O
Banned: EU* Banned: UK* Allowed: USA

*Requires warning label "may have adverse effect on activity and attention in children"

Brominated Veg. Oil
Found in: Mountain Dew · Some Citrus Sports Drinks
Banned: EU Banned: Japan Banned: India Allowed: USA

Bromine accumulates in body tissue. FDA banned it in 2024 — but it's still in supply chains.

Titanium Dioxide
Found in: Skittles · Chewing Gum · Frosting · Ranch Dressing
Banned: France 2020 Banned: EU 2022 Allowed: USA

Used purely for whitening appearance. EFSA found it can no longer be considered safe.

Potassium Bromate
Found in: Bread · Rolls · Bagels · Pizza Dough
Banned: EU Banned: UK Banned: Canada Allowed: USA

IARC classifies it as a possible human carcinogen. Banned in over 20 countries.

BHA / BHT
Found in: Cereal · Potato Chips · Chewing Gum · Butter
Banned: Japan Restricted: EU Allowed: USA

BHA listed as "reasonably anticipated to be a human carcinogen" by US Dept of Health.

TBHQ
Found in: Pop-Tarts · Ramen Noodles · McDonald's Nuggets
Banned: Japan Banned: EU Allowed: USA

Derived from petroleum. Banned in EU. High doses linked to tumors in animal studies.
